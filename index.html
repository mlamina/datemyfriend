The mechanics

Why this works.

  • —Your friends are the filter. They only show your QR to people they'd actually want to introduce you to.
  • —You don't have to swipe. You don't have to open the app. Your friends are out in the world meeting people for you.
  • —Your friends actually want you happy. They have skin in the game — the algorithm doesn't.
  • —Introductions happen in real life. A party, a dinner, a hike — the way it worked before apps and feeds and looksmaxxing.
  • —No stranger ever sees your profile. No public link, no discovery feed, no screenshots leaked into a group chat.
  • —You decide who gets your number. Notes come in. You text whoever you want. Nobody else gets through.
A note from the founder

I'm Marco. I met my partner on a dating app — so I know they can work. But getting there was genuinely draining: the endless swiping, the performance of keeping a public profile, the slow sense that meeting people had stopped feeling like meeting people. Most of my friends describe the same thing.

Somewhere along the way we handed all of this to an algorithm, and a lot of us are quietly tired of it. Your friends already know you — what makes you laugh, what you can't sit with, who broke your heart and exactly how. That isn't something a feed can learn.

So here's the idea: no public profile, no swiping, no strangers scrolling past you. Just a code your friends can show the people they'd actually want you to meet, and a few honest words they write about you.

This doesn't exist yet. The page you're reading is the whole of it — I'm trying to find out whether anyone else feels what I felt, and would actually want a thing like this. If that's you, the next part is a question, not a sign-up.

Marco

— Marco, San Francisco
